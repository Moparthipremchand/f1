TAJ-MAHAL

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, is a white marble mausoleum built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It's renowned for its intricate architecture and considered a symbol of love. The Taj Mahal is also one of the Seven Wonders of the World and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

GOLDEN TEMPLE

The Golden Temple, or Harmandir Sahib, is a sacred Sikh pilgrimage site in Amritsar, Punjab, known for its stunning gold-covered dome and its spiritual significance for Sikhs. It was built around a sacred pool called the Amrit Sarovar and is a symbol of Sikh values like equality and service, with its famous community kitchen serving millions annually

KAASHI

Kashi, also known as Varanasi, is one of the oldest living cities in the world and a deeply sacred pilgrimage site for Hindus, believed to offer salvation and liberation from the cycle of rebirth.

ARAKU VALLEY

Araku Valley is a beautiful hill station in Andhra Pradesh, India, known for its lush coffee plantations, tribal culture, and scenic landscapes. It offers a tranquil escape with attractions like the Borra Caves, Tribal Museum, and various waterfalls and gardens. The valley is nestled in the Eastern Ghats and boasts a moderate climate, making it a popular destination for tourists.
